Advanced Generative AI Program Curriculum

A Comprehensive Journey into Generative AI: From Fundamental Principles to Advanced Applications and Real-World Innovation


Module - I: Introduction to AI (4 hours)


Overview of AI

  •   AI Evolution and History
  •   Understanding AI Capabilities and Limitations
  •   AI vs. Machine Learning vs. Deep Learning
  •   AI Myths and Misconceptions

Module - II: AI for Non-Technical Audiences (4 hours)


  •   Simplified Explanation of AI Concepts
  •   Ethics and Governance in AI
  •   Basics of AI Algorithms and Data-Driven Decision-Making
  •   AI Applications in Everyday Life

Module - III: Applications of AI Across Industries (6 hours)


  •   AI in Healthcare, Finance, Retail, Manufacturing, and Other Sectors
  • Case Studies and Real-World Examples
  • Identifying AI Opportunities (Interactive Session)

Module IV: Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) (6 hours)


  • Introduction to Generative AI
  • Survey of LLMs (GPT, BERT, T5, LLama, Gemini, Mistral, Perplexity)
  • Hands-on Demonstration (Text, Image, and Media Generation)

Module V: : Python Programming


  • Basics of Python Programming
  • Control Flow and Decision Making
  • Loops in Python
  • Functions in Python
  • Python Data Structure
  • Working with Strings
  • File Handling in Python
  • Introduction to Modules and Libraries

Module VI: : Project-Based Learning (8 hours)


  • Project 1: Creative AI (Storyboarding & Video Generation)
  • Project 2: AI in Data Analysis
  • Project 3: AI-Driven ERP Solution for Retail

Module VII: Approaches to AI Solutioning (4 hours)


  • Frameworks for AI Solutioning
  • AI in Product Management
  • Agile Methodologies and Design Thinking for AI

Module VIII: Introduction to Robotic Process Automation (RPA) (4 hours)


  • Overview of RPA and its Business Applications
  • AI-Powered RPA and Intelligent Automation
  • Hands-on: Building Simple RPA Bots

Module IX: Emerging Trends and Future of AI (4 hours)


  • Explainable AI and Ethical AI
  • AI in IoT and Edge Computing
  • Capstone Discussion and Q&A

Module X: AI Ethics and Responsible AI (6 hours)


  • Introduction to AI Ethics: Principles and Guidelines
  • Bias in AI: Detection, Mitigation, and Case Studies
  • Explainability and Interpretability in AI Models
  • Privacy and Data Security in AI Systems
  • Ethical AI Governance Frameworks (EU AI Act, IEEE Standards, etc.)
  • Hands-on Session: Evaluating AI Systems for Bias and Fairness

Module XI: Prompt Engineering (4 hours)


  • Understanding Prompts and Their Role in A
  • Types of Prompting: Zero-Shot, One-Shot, Few-Shot Learning
  • Advanced Techniques: Chain-of-Thought (CoT), SelfConsistency, Tree of Thoughts
  • Hands-on Exercises: Enhancing Model Outputs with Effective Prompts

Module XII: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) (6 hours)


  • Introduction to RAG and How It Works
  • Integrating Vector Databases for Efficient Search (e.g., FAISS, Pinecone)
  • Implementing RAG in LLM Applications
  • Hands-on Project: Building a Q&A System with RAG

Module XIII: Fine-Tuning LLMs (6 hours)


  • Understanding Model Fine-Tuning vs. Pretraining
  • Data Preparation and Dataset Selection for Fine-Tuning
  • Tools for Fine-Tuning: Hugging Face Transformers, LoRA, QLoRA
  • Hands-on: Fine-Tuning a Pretrained LLM on Custom Data

Module XIV: AI Agents and Autonomous Systems (6 hours)


  • Introduction to AI Agents and Autonomous Systems
  • Multi-Agent Systems and Reinforcement Learning for AI Agents
  • Tools for AI Agents: LangChain, Auto-GPT, BabyAGI
  • Hands-on: Building an AI Agent for Automating Tasks

Final Capstone Project (8 hours)


  Students apply their learning to create a real-world AI solution

  Options:

  • AI-powered Chatbot with RAG
  • AI Agent for Workflow Automation
  • Fine-Tuned LLM for Industry-Specific Applications

  Presentation and Evaluation
